Benchmark Methodology

Test Sets

The model is evaluated on three independent splits derived from PLSDB v2025:

  • Validation (n=286): Used for early stopping and calibration.
  • Test (n=296): Held during development; used for final metric reporting.
  • Held-out (n=1,200): Sequestered from all training, validation and calibration; reported only in the final benchmark.

Splits use 21-mer Jaccard-aware clustering (threshold ≥ 0.85) to prevent near-duplicate leakage between splits. The held-out set is never used for training, calibration, or early stopping decisions.

Evaluation Protocol

  • Mobility: Balanced accuracy (macro-average of per-class recall across 3 classes).
  • AMR / Expansion: Area Under the ROC Curve (AUROC).
  • Task Score: Unweighted mean of the three metrics above.
  • Confidence intervals: Bootstrap with 500 resamples, 95% CI.
  • Calibration: L-BFGS temperature + bias scaling on validation logits.
  • Ensemble: v14 (weight 0.47) + v15 (weight 0.53) — weighted probability averaging.
  • Production inference: Reverse-complement averaged (original + revcomp, then averaged) — used by dna predict and dna interpret.
  • Benchmark inference (numbers above): Pre-cached features evaluated without RC averaging, matching the model-selection protocol for fast, reproducible benchmarks. RC-averaged numbers are ~1–2 points higher on the held-out split; regenerate with dna-sentinel benchmark --rc-average --ensemble-checkpoint .... Reports include an inference_mode field.

Baseline Numbers

Baseline metrics for DNABERT, DNABERT-2, and Nucleotide Transformer are estimated from published evaluations on comparable plasmid classification tasks and are not exact reproductions. The PLSDB baseline uses a logistic regression on 4–6 mer k-mer counts (same features as Cassiopeia).

Key differences: Cassiopeia Prime is explicitly designed for a 2,048-plasmid training cap. DNABERT and Nucleotide Transformer were pretrained on millions of sequences; their reported numbers reflect fine-tuning on the same limited data and may be lower than their full-data potential.

Known Limitations

  • Evidence windows are model attention scores, not experimentally validated breakpoints. They indicate which regions the model finds informative, not confirmed oriT or resistance genes.
  • The model does not run BLAST, gene calling, host metadata lookup, or assembly quality checks. Predictions are purely sequence-based.
  • Reverse-complement averaging reduces orientation instability but does not eliminate circular cut-point drift entirely.
  • The 2,048-plasmid training cap means the model is inherently data-constrained. Performance on rare or novel plasmid families may be lower.
  • Mobility classification (especially the “mobilizable” class) remains the most challenging task, with class 1 recall around 76% on held-out data.
  • Geographic expansion is a proxy based on observed spread to ≥15 countries. It does not account for sampling bias, reporting lag, or plasmid extinction.
  • AMR signatures are k-mer pattern based, not gene-level annotations. HIGH confidence does not guarantee the presence of specific resistance genes.
  • The model should not be used as the sole basis for clinical, environmental, regulatory, or biosafety decisions. Outputs require orthogonal validation.
